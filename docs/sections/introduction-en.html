Introduction back to ToC

In the modern scholarly ecosystem, academic publication data is highly fragmented across multiple isolated platforms, formats, and relational databases. This fragmentation complicates comprehensive bibliometric tracking, citation network analysis, and cross-institutional collaboration analysis. The Academic Research Publication Ontology (ARPO) addresses these challenges by establishing a structured, logically consistent OWL 2 DL vocabulary that allows for granular modeling and reasoning over complex academic publishing metadata.

Phase 1 of ARPO laid the baseline groundwork for core domain concepts including authors, publishers, venues, and explicit, irreflexive citation tracking. Phase 2 extends this core schema significantly by introducing advanced metadata handling via the Modular Ontology Modeling (MOMo) framework. This structural expansion incorporates reified, time-bounded affiliation records, controlled vocabularies for various open access classification standards, and explicit modeling of research funding structures and peer-review tracks.

Ultimately, ARPO is designed to act as the core schematic layer for an automated data integration pipeline. By leveraging advanced Language Models (LLMs) to reliably extract entities and properties directly from unstructured text strings and open bibliographic REST APIs (such as Semantic Scholar and OpenAlex), ARPO enables the deterministic creation of rich, queryable, and logically sound academic knowledge graphs.
